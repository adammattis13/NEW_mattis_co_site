The Opportunity We See

Local service businesses—septic pumping, HVAC repair, pest control, small utility services—are the invisible backbone of every community. They're cash generative but operationally inefficient: paper schedules, manual dispatching, fragmented customer records, and owners working 70-hour weeks.

Traditional roll-ups treat these as financial assets to optimize. We see them as community institutions to strengthen. Every septic company, every pest control route, every HVAC technician represents relationships built over decades. Our job is to make those relationships work better for everyone involved.

Our thesis: This is a local roll-up game with empathy, not just spreadsheets. We preserve what makes these businesses trusted in their communities while applying operational improvements that reduce stress, increase efficiency, and create sustainable growth.

Our Philosophy

"Every business we acquire has spent years earning community trust. Our job isn't to change that—it's to help them serve their communities better, treat their teams fairly, and build sustainable value that lasts beyond any exit timeline."

Case Study: Regional Septic & Utility Consolidation

How we transformed cash-generative but operationally inefficient local services into a streamlined, community-serving operation.

The Challenge

We acquired four independent businesses across a rural region: two septic pumping companies, a pest control service, and a small utility maintenance firm. Each was profitable but operationally chaotic—handwritten schedules, manual routing, and owners who hadn't taken a real vacation in years.

Technicians were driving inefficient routes, customers had no visibility into service windows, and seasonal demand spikes created chaos. The businesses were cash generative but burning out their owners and leaving customer satisfaction on the table.

The Solution

We implemented unified scheduling and routing systems while preserving each company's local identity and customer relationships. AI-powered route optimization reduced fuel costs by 30%, while automated customer communications eliminated missed appointments.

Critical principle: we kept every local phone number, every established customer relationship, and every trusted technician. The goal was to make their lives easier, not replace them with corporate efficiency.
