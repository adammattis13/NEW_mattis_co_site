Technology That Serves People

Most tech disrupts for disruption's sake. We invest in technology that actually helps people do better work, earn more money, and build stronger communities. Dispatch platforms for electricians. Scheduling software for home health aides. Route optimization for plumbers. AI that amplifies human skill, not replacement thinking.

Blue-collar workers represent 70+ million Americans earning a collective $3+ trillion annually. They run local economies, maintain essential infrastructure, and provide services every community needs. Yet Silicon Valley treats them as a "legacy workforce" to automate away.

Our thesis: The future belongs to technology that makes skilled workers more productive, better paid, and more connected to their communities. We invest in platforms that put human dignity first and economic opportunity second—because the two reinforce each other.

Our Core Belief

"The best technology doesn't replace human expertise—it amplifies it. A good plumber with smart scheduling and AI-assisted diagnostics isn't being automated away. They're becoming a better plumber who serves more customers, earns more money, and goes home to their family earlier."

Case Study: Regional Trades Dispatch Platform

How we backed a platform that transformed blue-collar work across three states while keeping workers and communities at the center.

The Challenge

Skilled tradespeople—electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs—were struggling with inconsistent work, inefficient routing, and exploitative platform fees. Existing platforms extracted 20-30% commissions while providing minimal value to workers or local economies.

Local businesses couldn't find reliable contractors when needed, leading to delayed projects and frustrated customers. Workers were driving inefficient routes, spending hours on administrative work, and competing on price rather than skill and service quality.

The Solution

We invested in a worker-owned cooperative platform with intelligent dispatching, skills-based matching, and transparent pricing. The platform takes a 5% fee (vs. industry standard 25%) and reinvests profits into worker benefits, training, and community development.

Key innovation: AI-powered scheduling that considers worker preferences, skill levels, customer location, and optimal routing. Workers maintain autonomy while getting better jobs, higher pay, and shorter commutes. Local businesses get reliable service from verified professionals.
